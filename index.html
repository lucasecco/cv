About Me

Always open for new opportunities and projects which can match and challenge my skills.

I attained my Federal VET Diploma(1) (CFC) in Information Technology in 2007 from the professional school of Lausanne EPSIC. During my five-year apprenticeship, I spent several months within the main IT departments of a multicultural and international company, acquiring practical IT-related skills and know-how such as Helpdesk, Proximity Support, Development, Quality, Systems Administration, Network and Telecommunication.

Lately, having accumulated a background of 10 years working as an IT Support Analyst for diverse non-governmental organizations and financial companies, and having a better understanding of the world we live in, I decided to expand my education and skills through a BSc (Honours) in Environmental Science, in which I have completed my second academic year in the fourth quarter of 2015. I am also following two specializations which are, Leading with Sense, and Project Planning and Management. I am now looking for a new professional challenge in IT and Environmental Science.

As both a person and professional, I like to tackle problems, I welcome new challenges, and I am always eager to learn new things. I am consistently praised for communicating effectively, and known for my excellent problem-solving skills and patience in helping both technical and nontechnical users.

During my free time I like to play soccer with friends, go running and to read.
